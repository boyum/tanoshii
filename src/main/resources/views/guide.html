üéØ Optimal Learning Workflows

Method 1: Write-First Approach

Best for: Handwriting learners who want maximum retention

Setup

  • Enable furigana (press F) if you're still learning kanji
  • Turn OFF "Show words" (press W) for maximum challenge
  • Have notebook + pen ready (use different colors for corrections)

Workflow

  1. Listen: Press Space to play audio 2-3 times
  2. Write: Hand-write what you hear in Japanese in your notebook
  3. Check: Press W to reveal the Japanese text
  4. Correct: Fix any mistakes in your notebook with a different color pen
  5. Translate: Write English translation in your notebook
  6. Verify: Type your translation and press Enter to check
  7. Vocabulary: Hover over words you didn't know, write them down
  8. Repeat: Review your handwritten corrections

Why This Works

Listening ‚Üí writing ‚Üí checking creates 3 memory touchpoints. Handwriting activates motor memory (proven to improve retention by 40-60%). Self-correction in different color highlights problem areas. The app provides instant verification without breaking your flow.

Method 2: Reading Comprehension + Writing

Best for: Visual learners who prefer reading first

Setup

  • Keep "Show words" ON (press W to toggle)
  • Disable furigana (press F) initially for challenge
  • Auto-play OFF (press A) to control pace

Workflow

  1. Read silently, try to guess the meaning
  2. Write translation in your notebook (English)
  3. Check with app: Type your translation, see feedback
  4. Write Japanese sentence 3 times in notebook (copying for practice)
  5. Vocabulary drill: Hover tooltips, write unknown words + meanings
  6. Close eyes and write: Try writing the sentence from memory
  7. Audio check: Press Space, follow along as you hear it

Why This Works

Reading ‚Üí writing ‚Üí copying ‚Üí recall builds progressive difficulty. Multiple exposures to same sentence in different modes. Handwriting reinforces kanji stroke order and character recognition.

Method 3: Story Immersion

Best for: Hard difficulty (3-4 sentence stories)

Setup

  • Select Hard difficulty for multi-sentence stories
  • Choose interesting topics from the 135 options
  • Furigana ON if needed (press F)

Workflow

  1. Listen to full story multiple times, eyes closed
  2. Write summary in English from memory
  3. Reveal text: Read Japanese with furigana
  4. Write full Japanese story in notebook (copying)
  5. Vocabulary extraction: Hover + write all new words with example sentence
  6. Hide text: Try to rewrite story from memory
  7. Audio loop: Listen again while reading your handwritten version

Why This Works

Context makes vocabulary stick better than isolated words. Story structure aids memory. Multiple modalities: audio ‚Üí reading ‚Üí writing ‚Üí recall creates deep encoding.

üéì Learning Science

Active Recall

Writing from memory is 3x more effective than passive reading. Force yourself to retrieve information rather than just recognizing it.

Multi-Modal Learning

Audio + visual + motor (writing) = 3x memory encoding. Each mode reinforces the others.

Immediate Feedback

App verification without 24-hour delay catches mistakes while they're fresh. Prevents bad habits from forming.

Spaced Repetition

Come back to the app daily. Review notebook weekly. The spacing effect improves long-term retention.

Contextual Learning

Topics + stories beat isolated vocabulary. Your brain remembers information better with context and narrative.

Motor Memory

Handwriting improves character recognition by 40-60%. Your hand remembers what your mind might forget.

üí° Pro Tips

1

Don't Rush

Better to do 5 sentences deeply than 20 superficially. Quality over quantity always wins in language learning.

2

Color Code Your Notebook

Blue (original writing), red (corrections), green (vocabulary notes). Visual cues help memory.

3

Speak Aloud While Writing

Adds auditory memory to motor memory. Even whispering helps reinforce pronunciation.

4

Use Topics Strategically

Pick topics relevant to your life or interests. You'll remember "cooking" vocabulary better if you love cooking.

5

Weekend Review

Every Sunday, rewrite your week's sentences from memory. This spaced repetition solidifies learning.

6

Shadow Writing

Advanced technique: Play audio and write in real-time as you hear it. Builds listening-writing connection.

7

Vocabulary Mining

After 10 tasks, hover every unfamiliar word. Create flashcards with the actual sentence from the app as context.

8

Track Progress Patterns

Review your corrections section weekly. If you keep making the same mistake, that's your focus for next week.

üìà Progress Tracking
